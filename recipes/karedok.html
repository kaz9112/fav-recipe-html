Karedok

Description

 Karedok is a mixture of vegetables that are combined with peanut sauce. The vegetables usually used are cabbage, bean sprouts, green round eggplant, long beans, and cucumber. Karedok can be considered as a Sundanese-style salad. Allegedly, karedok was originally a food served by the people to the regent.

 The making process and appearance of karedok looks similar to gado-gado. However, they are still different. Vegetables in gado-gado are boiled first. Unlike karedok which is served raw. But of course, the vegetables must be washed first. In addition, karedok also uses lesser galangal as the basic seasoning.

 Apart from karedok, there is also another raw vegetable dish served. This dish from Central Java is called terancam. The difference is, terancam does not use peanut sauce, but rather coconut sauce.

Ingredients

  • Bean sprouts, cleaned - 50 grams
  • Long beans, cleaned and thinly sliced - 50 grams
  • Cabbage, cleaned and finely sliced - 50 grams
  • Basil leaves, cleaned - 50 grams
  • Green round eggplant, cleaned and thinly sliced - 2 pieces
  • Cucumber, cleaned and thinly sliced into cubes - 1 piece
  • Peanuts, fried - 150 grams
  • Brown sugar - 50 grams
  • Garlic - 1 clove
  • Red chili - 2 pieces
  • Kaempferia galanga - 4 cm
  • Shrimp paste, roasted - 1/2 tsp
  • Tamarind water - 2 tablespoons
  • Salt - 1 tsp
  • Water - 75 ml

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, prepare the ground spices. Mix with water and stir until it becomes a paste.
  2. Add all the vegetables. Stir until well combined.
  3. Ready to serve.
